FAQs

Discover answers to common questions about our notary services and the Estate Planning process.

What is a notary?

A notary is a public official who is authorized to witness the signing of documents. They verify the identity of the signers and ensure that the documents are signed willingly. Notaries play a crucial role in preventing fraud and ensuring the integrity of important transactions.

How does Estate Planning work?

Estate Planning involves preparing essential documents like Powers of Attorney and Living Wills. Our process includes gathering client input, preparing the necessary documents, and conducting in-person notarization. This ensures that your wishes are clearly documented and legally binding.

What documents do I need?

The documents required depend on your specific needs and the type of notarization. Generally, you will need identification and any relevant documents you wish to have notarized. Our team will guide you through the necessary paperwork during your appointment.

Do you offer discounts?

Yes, we offer a military discount to honor those who serve. Please mention your military status when scheduling your appointment. Our goal is to provide accessible services to all members of the community.

How can I schedule?

You can easily schedule an appointment through our online appointment request form. Simply fill out the required information, and we will confirm your appointment promptly. This minimizes phone calls and streamlines the process for your convenience.
